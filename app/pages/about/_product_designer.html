Product Designer

We're looking for an experienced digital product designer to help shape the way organisations take recurring payments.

We're also always looking for talented freelancers to come and work with us, so if this sounds interesting but you're not looking for a full-time gig, get in touch by emailing tomp@gocardless.com.

Our work

Over the past few years, GoCardless' products have allowed small companies to take subscriptions for the first time — fundamentally changing the way they do business. But we're not done yet. We feel like we've just scratched the surface for small companies, and now we want to do the same thing for the biggest organisations on the planet.

We have a roadmap packed full of projects that will help us create a platform that let's anyone control their recurring payments like never before. This includes reimagining how enterprise software should work and be experienced by both employees and their customers.

Our culture

With a small, but talented team and an amazing new office in central London, we feel like we're in a great position to tackle these challenges. You will play a central role in shaping our team and culture, as well as the direction of our product. You'll work with a tight, talented group of designers and engineers, as well as the founders of GoCardless.

Despite the scale of our work, we're a small and friendly team with strong shared values. We get on well and enjoy spending time together, whether working in the office, chatting over lunch or out for drinks after work. We're passionate about learning, and we all care deeply about the quality of what we make.

We want to build a strong design culture at GoCardless, and you'll be key in helping us do that. We'll support you by paying for training, events, and sending you to conferences around the world if that's what you're into.

What we're looking for

We're looking for someone with real experience designing digital products that people rely on. You think about systems, not just screens, and the end user experience is your top priority.

Day to day, the job will be part interaction design, part web design, part product strategy. We work collaboratively across disciplines on hard, interesting problems. Sometimes you'll be speaking to our users, other times you'll be crafting brand-led materials to help tell our story. No two days will be the same, but we want each one to be exciting!

Main responsibilities:

  • Contributing to the design of our products for both small and large merchants
  • Speaking to users to identify the problems and opportunities that lie ahead
  • Leading some projects, and managing small teams of designers and developers
  • Producing work for our public facing website, as well as some marketing materials
  • Pushing the team to consistently raise the bar for visual design and simplicity

Ideal requirements:

  • Experience shipping digital products and improving them over time
  • Designed and led user research / testing sessions
  • Can implement some of your work using HTML / CSS
  • Have used Git / GitHub for version control across teams
  • A strong portfolio of relevant work
